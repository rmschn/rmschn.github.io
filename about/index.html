About Ray

Builder. Engineer. Veteran

I build systems that last.

Not just because they scale, but because they’re designed with clarity, resilience, and purpose. My work lives at the intersection of deep technical craft and real-world impact—where code meets complexity, and simplicity is hard-won.

For over two decades, I’ve helped large organizations modernize legacy infrastructure, automate fragile processes, and design platforms that empower engineers instead of slowing them down. I’ve led technical transformations in finance, telecommunications, and cybersecurity—turning opaque, high-risk systems into secure, observable, and sustainable ones.

I’ve replaced million-dollar tooling with open, automated alternatives. Built platforms handling millions of transactions per second. Created real-time visibility for leaders who need to know, not guess. And designed security systems that detect threats not just by rules, but by reasoning.

Along the way, I’ve mentored engineers, led remote teams, and helped launch products that customers rely on. But my focus has always been the same: solve hard problems once, so others don’t have to solve them again.

Before all this, I served in the U.S. Marine Corps. That’s where I learned that the best solutions aren’t always the most complex—just the clearest, most dependable under pressure. That mindset still guides me: mission-first, team-centered, built to endure.

Now, I’m exploring the next frontier: autonomous development, localized AI, and how we can give engineers more agency, less toil, and better tools—without sacrificing security or sanity.

This site is a record of what I’ve built, what I’m learning, and what I believe about technology, systems, and the people who make them work.
